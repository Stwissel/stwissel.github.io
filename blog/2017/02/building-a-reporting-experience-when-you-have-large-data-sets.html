Building a reporting experience when you have large data sets

Everybody likes responsive systems, thus in every software specification one finds statements like " must be responsive" or " shall not exceed 3 seconds response time". This isn't just a fidget of the NOW generation, but a well established finding. But there are things that simply take time. Running analysis or reports on large data sets is one of them. After all the "mother of big data Hadoop is at its core a batch processing system.
To solve that seemingly contradiction between reality and requirement, you need to take a step back and decompose your solution.

Response Time != Processing Time

When you order your new Tesla the total processing time might take a few month, but you won't eagerly sit in front of the order screen (maybe you do) and look at a spinning wheel until a message pops up: "Order delivered". When you send a tweet from you mobile app in a really crowded location, like SMRT at 8am with 1000 devices fighting for network in a metal tube, you don't wait until the message is transmitted. You continue reading, sending happens in the background.
At the core of these experience is the pattern of "asynchronous processing" with long (Tesla) and short (Twitter) running queues. Getting asynchronous right is hard. The first hurdle is to explain it to people who write requirements. It is easy to say "No, I don't want a queue, I want real time" and then freak out over the cost implications. The second challenge: real time is very appealing and in reporting the ability to progressively filter down is a brilliant interaction model. So drawing the line between real time interaction and batch submission isn't clear cut or has "the one" solution. A well designed system "knows" when to say: "Got it, let me get back to you"

Decompose your steps

Each reporting, be it a dashboard, an interactive on screen report or torture of dead trees follows the same pattern:
  1. Selection / Definition of criteria
  2. Processing of the result set
  3. Visualisation of the result
The middle step mostly is the most computing intensive, so it is a candidate for background processing (Ajax call anyone). In highly interactive reporting (Drill Down Dashboards) all steps are tightly coupled and a background processing won't improve the user experience due to the interactive expectations. Won't improve unless you make the system a learning one.
